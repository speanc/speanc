A

Actus Reus

the physical component of an offence. Indicated by action words like applies, steals, damages, destroys, obstructs, etc.


B

Breach

jargon for failing to abide by legal conditions (e.g., the offender breached his condition not to consume drugs)


C

Community Supervision

a general term that includes any court-ordered disposition that is fulfilled entirely in the community, with no custodial component.

Compulsory Conditions

the conditions that must be imposed on all probation orders, as per S. 732.1(2) of the Criminal Code

Custodial Sentence

a sentence of imprisonment, where the offender is held ‘in custody’.

Conditional Release

either (a) an early release from custody (e.g., parole, statutory release); or (b) direction that a custodial sentence (or portion thereof) be served in the community according to specific conditions.


D

Dangerous offender

a designation that can be assigned to an offender by the judge at sentencing, as per the criteria in Part XXIV of the Criminal Code. According to the Criminal Code, offenders serving long-term supervision orders are also designated dangerous offenders. As per S. 753(4), the court has a range of sentencing options for dangerous offenders, including an indeterminate sentence.

Day Parole

the authority granted to an offender by the Parole Board of Canada or a provincial parole board to be at large during the offender’s sentence in order to prepare the offender for full parole or statutory release, the conditions of which require the offender to return to a penitentiary, community-based residential facility, provincial correctional facility or other location each night or at another specified interval.

Detention

the act of detaining an offender (or keeping them incarcerated) beyond their statutory release date.

Discretion

the ability to choose between several courses of action that all exist within specific parameters (e.g., a judge has significant discretion in sentencing as long as they remain between the minimum and maximum allowable sentences).


E

Earned Remission

the term for the number of days deducted from a provincial inmate’s custodial sentence for “good behavior” (i.e., by following institutional rules). The maximum amount of earned remission works out to be approximately 1/3 of their sentence.

Eligibility Date

the date upon which an offender becomes eligible for day or full parole. Offenders are not necessarily released on their eligibility dates; rather, this date indicates the beginning of a period of time during which they can apply for, and be granted, a day or full parole release.


F

Full Parole

the authority granted to an offender by the Parole Board of Canada or a provincial parole board to be at large during the offender’s sentence.


G

General conditions

the term used to describe the conditions that apply to all offenders on parole or statutory release, as per s. 161 of the Corrections and Conditional Release Act.


I

Intermittent Sentence

a custodial sentence that can be served in chunks of time, often on weekends. Intermittent sentences only apply to sentences of 90 days or less.


L

Lifers

jargon for someone serving a life (indeterminate) sentence (i.e., the offender remains under sentence for the duration of their life)

Long-term Supervision Order

a supervision order that is imposed by the judge at sentencing as per S. 753(4), S. 753.01(5) or (6) or S. 753.1(3) or S. 759(3)(a)(i) of the Criminal Code. A long-term supervision order comes into effect following a custodial sentence of 2 years or more and can be imposed for a maximum length of 10 years.


M

Mens Rea

mental intent, guilty mind. Indicated by words like wilfully, knowingly, fraudulently, with intent, etc.


O

Optional Conditions

conditions that can be imposed on probations orders, based on an individualized assessment of each offender’s risk/needs. Judge’s can choose from the optional conditions listed in S. 732.1(3) and S. 732.1(3.1) of the Criminal Code.


P

Programs

this term refers to a variety of treatment, correctional, or rehabilitative programs that offenders participate in during their sentence. Programs are offered in correctional facilities and in the community.

Prohibition

a restriction of behavior that is enforced by law.


R

Recidivism

reoffending by a convicted offender; the tendency of an offender to reoffend in the future.


S

Sentence Management

a department within a correctional facility that is responsible for all sentence calculations, including aggregate sentence length and release dates. Sentence management is also responsible for managing other sentencing aspects, such as court dates, new convictions, prohibition orders, and restitution orders.

Special/Specific Conditions

these terms are sometimes used interchangeably and refer to the conditions that are recommended by CSC to be imposed on an offender’s parole or statutory release, based on an individualized risk assessment. These conditions vary for each offender.

Statutory Release

release from imprisonment subject to supervision before the expiration of an offender’s sentence, to which an offender is entitled under S. 127 of the Corrections and Conditional Release Act. Statutory release is mandatory by law and occurs at 2/3 of the offender’s sentence.

Surety

a guarantee that an accused will follow conditions and attend court. A surety can be material goods (e.g., money, property) or it can take the form of a person the court deems responsible and trustworthy, guaranteeing the behavior and appearance of the accused.


T

Tribunal

a person or group of people who have the authority to make legally-binding decisions in specific circumstances, for specific groups of people, or in certain types of cases. For example, the Parole Board of Canada is an administrative tribunal with authority over release decisions regarding provincial and federal offenders.


W

Warrant Expiry Date

the end of a court-imposed sentence of imprisonment; the date that their “Warrant of Committal” to a correctional facility expires.

Warrant of Committal

the legal document that commits an offender to custody in a correctional facility.
